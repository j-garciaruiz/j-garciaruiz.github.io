        <h2 class="section-title">About</h2>
        <div class="about-content">
            <p>Johnattan Garc&iacute;a-Ruiz, MPH, MBA, LL.B., is a Department Associate affiliated with the Takemi Program in International Health at the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health Department of Global Health and Population. His work focuses on helping people in Latin America and the Caribbean access the medicines and health technologies they need&mdash;through consulting, research, teaching, and design. He works across policy, global health, innovation, and digital communications. He is also a Lecturer at the Universidad de los Andes School of Law and the Universidad del Rosario School of Medicine and Health Sciences in Bogota, Colombia.</p>

            <p>As Co-founder and Managing Director of <a href="https://decilion.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Decilion</a>, a global health consultancy, Johnattan leads projects at the intersection of comparative health systems research and digital storytelling for international health organizations, top global health academic centers, and highly cited academic journals. He oversees a multidisciplinary team delivering projects in global health research, digital strategy, and data visualization. He has consulted with leading international organizations including the Inter-American Development Bank (IDB), Expertise France, the Pan American Health Organization (PAHO), the Economic Commission for Latin America and the Caribbean (ECLAC), Oxfam International, the World Health Organization, ACCESS Health International, and the U4 Anti-Corruption Resource Centre. His recent work includes evaluating policies and market dynamics for generic and biosimilar medicines, performing comparative analyses of regulatory agencies, assessing COVID-19 vaccine distribution inequities, and developing strategies to strengthen local drug manufacturing in Latin America.</p>

            <p>He received his Law degree (LL.B.) from Universidad de los Andes in Bogota, Colombia (thesis: <em>Social Rights and Determinants of Health: Closing the Gap</em>), a Master of Public Health (MPH) degree in Global Health from Harvard University, and an MBA from Sa&iuml;d Business School, University of Oxford, where he was a member of the Social Impact Lab at the Skoll Centre for Social Entrepreneurship.</p>

            <p>Earlier in his career, Johnattan served as legal advisor to the Director of Medicines and Health Technologies at the Colombian Ministry of Health and Social Protection, focusing on drug access and price regulation. He co-founded the Environmental and Public Health Law Clinic at Universidad de los Andes, where he participated as amicus curiae for the Colombian Constitutional Court on pharmaceutical policy cases. He has also held positions at the Harvard Health Systems Innovation Lab, the Center for the Study of Law, Justice and Society (Dejusticia), the Center for Global Development, and the WHO Centre for Health Development in Kobe, Japan.</p>

            <p>His research interests include global health, access to medicines and health technologies, comparative health systems, health reforms, health economics, health technology assessment, and the use of digital technologies to communicate public health information.</p>

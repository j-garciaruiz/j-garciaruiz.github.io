Publications & Research

Academic publications on global health, pharmaceutical policy, health systems, and access to medicines

My research focuses on understanding and improving health systems in low- and middle-income countries, with particular emphasis on pharmaceutical policy, access to medicines, and the role of law and regulation in achieving universal health coverage.

Recent Publications (2020-2024)

2024

The Evolution of Health Benefits Packages in Colombia: Thirty Years of Successes and Failures

Brun Vergara M, Garcia Ruiz J, Guzman J

Health Systems & Reform, 2024

Examines how Colombia's health benefits packages evolved from two explicit packages to an implicit approach covering everything for everyone, with lessons for other LMICs pursuing universal health coverage. Analyzes thirty years of policy evolution, implementation challenges, and outcomes.

Health Systems Universal Coverage Colombia
2022

Our Voice in the Ciclovia: Exercising Recreation and Health Rights through Citizen Science

Higuera-Mendieta D, Gonzalez SA, Garcia Ruiz J, et al.

Cities & Health, 2022

Explores the use of citizen science methodologies to document and advocate for urban health interventions, using Bogota's Ciclovia as a case study for understanding the intersection of public space, recreation, and health rights.

Urban Health Citizen Science Health Rights
2021

Decriminalizing HIV in Colombia

Duran Silva S, Garcia Ruiz J, et al.

Harvard Public Health Review (HPHR), Issue 38, 2021

Argues that HIV criminalization is counterproductive and undermines public health goals. Examines the Colombian context and proposes alternative public health approaches that prioritize treatment, prevention, and destigmatization over criminalization.

HIV/AIDS Public Health Law Colombia

Earlier Publications (2013-2019)

2016

Closing the Gap Between Formal and Material Health Care Coverage in Colombia

Lamprea E, Garcia J

Health and Human Rights, 18(2), pp. 49-65, 2016

Explores Colombia's path toward universal health coverage, distinguishing between formal legal coverage and material access to health services. Analyzes the gap between de jure rights and de facto access, and examines the role of constitutional litigation in closing this gap.

Universal Coverage Health Rights Access to Care
2013

The Judicialization of Health in Colombia

Bernal O, Velasco S, Garcia Ruiz J, et al.

Proceedings in GV - Global Virtual Conference, 1(1), pp. 310-313, 2013

Examines the phenomenon of health judicialization in Colombia, where citizens use constitutional courts to access medicines and health services. Analyzes the impact of judicial intervention on health system governance and resource allocation.

Health Litigation Judicial Activism Health Governance
